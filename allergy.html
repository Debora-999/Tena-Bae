If someone is experiencing an allergic reaction, follow these steps:
1. Assess the Situation
Determine the severity of the reaction. Symptoms can range from mild to severe (anaphylaxis).
2. Mild Reaction
Symptom Management: If symptoms include mild itching, hives, or sneezing:
Administer antihistamines (if available and the person is not allergic to them).
Keep the person calm and comfortable.
3. Severe Reaction (Anaphylaxis)
Call Emergency Services: Dial emergency services immediately.
Administer Epinephrine: If the person has an auto-injector (EpiPen):
Administer the injection into the outer thigh, holding it in place for about 10 seconds.
Positioning: Lay the person down and elevate their legs if possible, unless they are having difficulty breathing. In that case, allow them to sit up.
4. Monitor Symptoms
Keep an eye on the person's breathing and consciousness. Be prepared to perform CPR if they become unresponsive.
5. Stay with the Person
Do not leave them alone. Provide reassurance and keep them calm until help arrives.
6. Follow-Up Care
Once emergency responders arrive, provide them with information about the allergen and any treatments given.
Important Notes
Avoid giving food or drink in case of severe reactions.
If the person has a known allergy, ensure they have an action plan in place for future reactions.
Stay calm and act quickly to help!

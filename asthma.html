Signs of an Asthma Attack
- Wheezing or coughing
- Shortness of breath
- Chest tightness or pain
- Difficulty talking or performing normal activities
- Rapid breathing
Immediate Actions
1. Stay Calm: Reassure the person and help them stay calm, as anxiety can worsen symptoms.
2. Sit Upright: Encourage the person to sit up straight to help open the airways.
3. Use an Inhaler:
- If the person has a prescribed rescue inhaler (bronchodilator), help them use it.
- Instruct them to take 1-2 puffs, waiting about 30 seconds between puffs.
4. Monitor Symptoms:
- If symptoms do not improve within 5-10 minutes after using the inhaler, repeat the dose if advised by a healthcare provider.
5. Seek Help:
- Call emergency services if:
- The inhaler does not relieve symptoms.
- The person has trouble speaking, or their breathing worsens.
- They are unable to function normally.
After an Attack
- Encourage the person to rest and monitor their symptoms.
- If they have a written asthma action plan, follow it for further management.
Important Notes
- Avoid triggers known to cause asthma attacks, such as smoke, allergens, and strong odors.
- Ensure the person has a spare inhaler and knows how to use it correctly.
Stay prepared and informed about asthma management!

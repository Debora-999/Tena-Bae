CPR

In an emergency situation requiring CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation), follow these steps:
1. Ensure Safety
Check the environment to ensure it is safe for you and the victim.
2. Check Responsiveness
Tap the person and shout, "Are you okay?"
If unresponsive, call for help immediately.
3. Call Emergency Services
If alone, call emergency services (e.g., 911) or ask someone else to call while you attend to the victim.
4. Check Breathing
Look, listen, and feel for breathing for no more than 10 seconds.
If the person is not breathing or only gasping, begin CPR.
5. Start Chest Compressions
Position: Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest, with the other hand on top.
Compress: Push down hard and fast at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute, allowing full chest recoil.
Depth: Compress at least 2 inches deep.
6. Open Airway
Tilt the head back slightly and lift the chin to open the airway.
7. Give Rescue Breaths (if trained)
Pinch the nose, cover the person’s mouth with yours, and give 2 breaths (1 second each), ensuring the chest rises.
After each breath, continue with 30 chest compressions.
8. Continue CPR
Alternate between 30 chest compressions and 2 rescue breaths.
Continue until emergency services arrive or the person shows signs of life.
9. Use an AED (if available)
If there’s an AED (Automated External Defibrillator) available, turn it on and follow the voice prompts.
10. Stay Calm
Keep calm and focused throughout the process. Your actions can save a life.
Important Notes
If you are untrained or unsure about giving rescue breaths, perform hands-only CPR (chest compressions only).
Always seek proper training for CPR and first aid to be prepared for emergencies.
Stay safe and be prepared!

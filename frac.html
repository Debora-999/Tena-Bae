Fracture

If you suspect someone has a fracture (broken bone), follow these steps for proper care:
Signs of a Fracture
Severe pain at the injury site
Swelling or bruising
Deformity or abnormal positioning of the limb
Inability to move the affected area
A "grating" sensation or sound when moving the bone
Immediate Actions
Stay Calm:
Ensure the injured person remains calm and still to prevent further injury.
Immobilize the Area:
If possible, keep the injured limb in the position you found it.
Use a splint or any rigid object (like a rolled-up newspaper) to stabilize the area without moving it.
Apply Ice:
Place ice (wrapped in a cloth) on the swollen area to reduce pain and swelling. Avoid direct contact with the skin.
Seek Medical Attention:
Call emergency services or transport the person to a medical facility for a proper evaluation and treatment.
Do Not:
Do not try to realign or push the bone back into place.
Avoid giving the person food or drink in case surgery is needed.
Important Notes
Monitor for signs of shock, such as rapid heartbeat, pale skin, or confusion.
Follow up with a healthcare provider for proper treatment and rehabilitation.
Stay safe and act quickly in case of fractures!

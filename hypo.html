Hypoglycemia

Hypoglycemia, or low blood sugar, can be a medical emergency. Here are the steps to take if someone is experiencing hypoglycemia:
Signs and Symptoms
Shakiness
Sweating
Confusion or irritability
Dizziness or lightheadedness
Hunger
Rapid heartbeat
Blurred vision
Immediate Actions
Recognize Symptoms: If someone shows signs of hypoglycemia, act quickly.
Check Blood Sugar (if possible): If they have a glucose meter, check their blood sugar level.
Give Fast-Acting Carbohydrates:
Provide 15-20 grams of fast-acting carbohydrates. Options include:
Glucose tablets (follow package instructions)
1/2 cup (4 oz) of fruit juice or regular soda
1 tablespoon of sugar or honey
8 oz of low-fat milk
Wait and Recheck:
Wait about 15 minutes and recheck their blood sugar.
If still low, give another 15-20 grams of carbohydrates.
Follow Up with a Snack:
Once blood sugar is back to normal, have them eat a longer-lasting carbohydrate (like a sandwich or a piece of fruit) to prevent another drop in blood sugar.
When to Seek Medical Help
If the person is unconscious, unable to swallow, or if symptoms do not improve after treatment.
Call emergency services immediately.
Important Notes
If the person has diabetes, they should have a glucagon kit available for severe hypoglycemia.
Make sure to educate those around you about recognizing and treating hypoglycemia.
Stay safe and be prepared!

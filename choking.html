Choking

In the event of a choking emergency, follow these steps:
1. Assess the Situation
Mild Choking: The person can cough, speak, or breathe. Encourage them to cough to expel the object.
Severe Choking: The person cannot breathe, speak, or is turning blue. They may clutch their throat.
2. Call for Help
If the person is severely choking, call emergency services immediately.
3. Perform Abdominal Thrusts (Heimlich Maneuver)
For Adults and Children Over 1 Year:
Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around their waist.
Make a fist with one hand and place it just above their navel.
Grasp your fist with your other hand and thrust inward and slightly upward.
Repeat until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious.
4. For Infants Under 1 Year
Back Blows and Chest Thrusts:
Hold the infant face down on your forearm, supporting their head.
Give 5 firm back blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand.
If the object does not come out, turn the infant face up.
Give 5 chest thrusts (using 2-3 fingers in the center of the chest).
Repeat until the object is expelled or the infant becomes unresponsive.
5. If the Person Becomes Unresponsive
Carefully lower them to the ground.
Start CPR immediately (30 compressions followed by 2 rescue breaths).
Check the mouth for the obstructing object before giving rescue breaths.
6. Continue Until Help Arrives
Keep performing the appropriate technique until emergency services arrive or the person recovers.
Important Notes
Do Not: Slap the back of a choking person who is standing or sitting as this may worsen the situation.
Seek Training: It's advisable to take first aid and CPR courses to be prepared for choking emergencies.
Stay calm and act quickly!
